    Understanding terms

    What is a trigger law?

    Roe V. Wade had prevented criminalization of abortion across the country. With the overturning of this federal law, previously unenforceable "trigger laws" passed in individual states can now be enforced. 13 states have trigger laws, but not all act in the same way.

    States: Arkansas, Idaho, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, Missouri, North & South Dakota, Oklahoma, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Wyoming

    What does it mean if a law is blocked?

    Post-Roe, roughly half of the states have sought to ban or severely restrict abortion. An influx of lawsuits, mainly from abortion rights groups, seek to:

    1. Block new bans with the argument that they are vague or violate state constitution, or

    2. Prevent old bans from going into effect by arguing that they are antiquated.

    Advocates have persuaded judges in Louisiana and Utah to temporarily block enforcement of trigger laws. Similar lawsuits are pending in Arizona, Kentucky, Mississippi and Idaho.

    What is gestational limit?

    Gestational limits determine the point within pregnancy when abortion is lawful In the US, these limits vary by state, and numerous states have passed laws that prohibit abortions after an early gestational age (measured from the first day of last menstrual period to current date). Some states protecting abortion also have limits, generally until viability (24-26 weeks), while others have no limits at all.
